The Calculations - Determining the "Z-Score"

Step One

The "z-score" is the number that represents the safety level of a school. Data regarding the number of on-campus murders, rapes, violent crimes, assaults, property crimes, and thefts were collected from FBI databases. In the equation, m = murders, r = rapes, v = violent crimes, a = assaults, p = property crimes, and t = thefts. The definition of rape used by the FBI includes revised and legacy rapes, as of 2013. The thefts is a sum of larceny, robbery, burglary, and motor vehicle theft. Each variable was assigned a respective coefficient that determined the weight of the crime. For example, murder has the greatest coefficient (km = 100) because it is a far more serious crime than theft, for example.

Step Two

The summation of the crimes was divided by e, which represents the enrollment size. The sum divided by the enrollment was then subtracted from 1. The higher the z-score, the more safe a school is, and the lower the z-score, the more unsafe it is. The scale for the z-score is from 0 to 1, and the average z-score is about 0.93 (B+).
